Health Analytics Dashboard

Advanced analysis of 14 years of Google health search trends across the United States

Research Overview

This comprehensive analysis examines the evolution of public interest in major health conditions through Google search data from 2004 to 2017. By correlating search trends with CDC mortality statistics, we uncover valuable insights into public health awareness, regional variations, and the relationship between information-seeking behavior and actual health outcomes.

Our research leverages advanced data science techniques to process over 1 million data points, providing unprecedented visibility into how Americans engage with health information online. The interactive visualizations below allow for deep exploration of patterns, correlations, and trends across nine major health conditions and fifty states.

Health Search Volume by State and Region

Geographic analysis reveals significant regional variations in health search behavior. Metropolitan areas like California, Texas, and New York show the highest engagement with online health information, while search patterns also reflect regional health concerns and awareness levels.

Trend of Change in Total Search Volume

The overall upward trend in health-related searches coincides with technological advancements and growing public reliance on telehealth and online health resources. The 240% increase from 2004 to 2017 reflects the digital transformation of healthcare information access.

Health Search Distribution Analysis

Distribution analysis shows significant variability in search patterns across different health conditions. Diabetes and depression demonstrate the widest ranges of public interest, suggesting diverse information needs and awareness levels among the population.

Search Trends by Health Condition

All monitored conditions show increasing search trends over the 14-year period, with cancer maintaining consistently high public interest. The parallel growth across conditions suggests a general increase in health information seeking rather than condition-specific concerns.

Health Search Patterns by State

Cancer emerges as the most searched health condition across all states, highlighting sustained public concern and information-seeking behavior. State-level variations reflect differences in population health profiles, awareness campaigns, and healthcare access.

Total Health Search Volume Distribution

Radar visualization confirms cancer's dominance in online health searches. Interestingly, search frequency doesn't always correlate directly with condition prevalence, suggesting complex relationships between public awareness, stigma, and information needs.

Correlations Among Health Conditions

Correlation analysis reveals significant relationships between search patterns for different conditions. Strong correlations between depression, diabetes, and obesity searches reflect the well-documented comorbidities and public awareness of these interconnected health issues.

Note: Correlation coefficients (r) range from -1 to 1, where values closer to 1 indicate stronger positive relationships. These correlations reflect search pattern similarities rather than causal relationships between health conditions.
